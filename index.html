Biography [Full CV]

I am currently a second-year graduate student at Robotics Institute, Carnegie Mellon University. I work with Prof. Emma Brunskill, Prof. Jeffrey Bigham, Prof. Niki Kittur and Prof. Chinmay Kulkarni. I graduated from Tsinghua University, with a B.E. in Computer Software and a B.A. in Economics.

Research Interests

My research interests lie in Machine Learning and Human-Computer Interaction, especially intelligent systems involving both. I am excited about exploring the potential to promote social good through effective intelligence. I am currently working on personalized education for arbitrary topics.

Projects

Questimator: Generating Knowledge Assessments for Arbitrary Topics

With Chinmay Kulkarni, Niki Kittur, Jeffrey Bigham and Emma Brunskill
(to appear in IJCAI 2016) An automated system that generates multiple-choice assessment questions for any topic contained within Wikipedia. Given a topic, Questimator traverses the Wikipedia graph to find and rank related topics, and uses article text to form both questions and distractor options.

Demo Website Preprint Code

Sentiment Analysis for Sparse Social Media Text

With Xiaoming Jin and Deli Zhao
A system that crawls short texts from Twitter and Weibo, gets their topic (LDA) weights and trains separate sentiment classifier for each topic and aggeragate to get final labels. Sentiment index was applied to behavioral finance, to find relations between stock market trend and public sentiments (Distinct Economics Thesis).

Digital Barber: 3D Hair Manipulation on Mobile Phone

With Fan Yang and Lvdi Wang
A mobile phone application that can generate and manipulate high-quality 3D hair & head models, based on single-image dense 3D hair strands reconstruction technique.
